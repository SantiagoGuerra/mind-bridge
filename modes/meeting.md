# Mode 6 — Prepare meeting

Activation: "prepare me for a meeting", "help me present this", "I need to explain X in a meeting" — ES: "prepárame para una reunión", "ayúdame a presentar esto", "necesito explicar X en reunión".

## Template

Load `templates/meeting-prep.md`.

## Calibration

Apply global calibration from SKILL.md. Communication modifier active by default. If the meeting informs a product decision, also activate the Product modifier.

## Initial questions (only the ones missing)

- What type of output: prior document, mental model for yourself, live response, or written follow-up?
- Who will be there? (Only if tone matters.)
- How much time do you have to speak?
- Is there a decision being made in that meeting or is it informational?
- **What is the success criterion for the meeting?** (convince / unblock a decision / explain risk / align team / sell idea)

Do not ask all 5. Ask only the ones missing from context. If everything is clear, skip the questions.

## Full structure

```
MEETING GOAL:
THESIS:
PROBLEM:
INSIGHT:
DECISION / PROPOSAL:
REASON:
TRADEOFF:
RISK:
EXAMPLE:
LIKELY OBJECTIONS:
RESPONSES:
WHAT I MUST NOT SAY:
NEXT STEP:
30-SECOND VERSION:
5-MINUTE VERSION:
```

## Sparring partner

After building the structure:

- Identify the **2–3 weakest parts** of the argument.
- Attack them as the most critical participant would.
- Propose how to reinforce them.

> "The weakest point is [X]. If someone asks [Y], the strongest answer is [Z]."

Do not skip this step. It is half the value of the mode.

## Anticipating objections

For each likely objection:

- Who would raise it
- Why they would raise it
- Short answer (1 sentence)
- Extended answer (if they press)

## Output types

**Prior document**: structured markdown, complete, sendable.

**Mental model for me**: loose bullets, visual shortcuts, no prose required.

**Live response**: one memorable sentence + 2–3 supporting points. No paper.

**Written follow-up**: summary + decision + next step. Tone according to audience.

## Final verification

- Does the 30-SECOND VERSION contain THESIS + main REASON in 3–4 sentences?
- Is WHAT I MUST NOT SAY explicit?
- Is there ONE concrete NEXT STEP?

## Mode output

- If the meeting is soon: close with the memorable 30s version.
- If there is time: chain Mode 4 (validate the argument) or Mode 5 (open a weak branch).
